# Known failure modes — short_term_reversal_1m

## 1. Momentum regimes

Short-term reversal is the opposite of momentum. During strong
momentum regimes (e.g. tech rally, sector rotation), losers keep
losing and winners keep winning. The strategy is structurally short
momentum and will underperform in these periods.

## 2. Microstructure noise

Bid-ask bounce and other microstructure effects inflate apparent
short-term reversal. In live trading with transaction costs, much
of the theoretical alpha is consumed by spreads and slippage,
especially in less liquid assets.

## 3. Earnings season disruptions

Stocks that drop on earnings often continue to drift lower as
analysts revise estimates. The strategy will buy these post-earnings
losers, mistaking a fundamental re-pricing for a temporary dislocation.

## 4. Narrow universe

Cross-sectional reversal requires dispersion across assets. With a
small universe (fewer than ~10 assets), the ranking is noisy and the
long/short portfolios are concentrated, leading to high idiosyncratic
risk.

# Known failure modes — fx_carry_em

## 1. Peso problems (rare tail events)

EM currencies are prone to "peso problems" — long periods of
stable carry returns punctuated by rare, catastrophic devaluations.
The carry trade can lose years of accumulated returns in days
during an EM currency crisis (e.g., TRY -40% in 2018, BRL -30%
in 2020).

## 2. EM capital controls

Emerging-market governments may impose capital controls that prevent
repatriation of carry returns or restrict currency convertibility
(e.g., India's NDF market, Malaysia's ringgit controls in 1998).
These controls can trap positions and create basis risk between
onshore and offshore rates.

## 3. Sovereign risk

EM carry strategies are exposed to sovereign default and
restructuring risk. Currency depreciation often accompanies
sovereign stress, amplifying carry-trade losses precisely when
the strategy is most vulnerable.

## 4. Higher bid-ask spreads

EM currency pairs have wider bid-ask spreads than G10 pairs,
especially during stress periods. Transaction costs can erode
a significant portion of carry returns, and liquidity can
evaporate during crises when positions most need to be unwound.

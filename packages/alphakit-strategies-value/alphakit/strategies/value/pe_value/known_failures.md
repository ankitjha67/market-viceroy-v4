# Known failure modes — pe_value

## 1. Negative earnings (P/E undefined)

When a firm reports negative earnings, the P/E ratio is undefined or
negative. The strategy's price-only proxy does not distinguish between
cheap profitable firms and loss-making firms with low prices.

## 2. Cyclical earnings bias

P/E ratios are distorted at business-cycle turning points. Earnings
collapse in recessions, inflating P/E for cyclical firms just when
they may be cheapest on a normalized basis. The trailing return proxy
partially inherits this problem.

## 3. Proxy conflates with reversal

The price-derived proxy (negative trailing 3-year return) captures
long-term reversal as well as the earnings-yield effect. Returns
attributed to "value" may partly reflect mean-reversion dynamics.

## 4. Sector concentration risk

Low P/E screens historically concentrate in financials, utilities,
and mature industrials. The strategy may carry uncompensated sector
risk, especially during sector-specific drawdowns (e.g. financials
in 2008).

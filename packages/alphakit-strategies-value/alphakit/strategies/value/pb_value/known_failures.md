# Known failure modes — pb_value

## 1. Value trap

Stocks with low P/B may be cheap for good reason — deteriorating
fundamentals, declining industries, or permanent impairment. The
strategy will go long these "value traps" and suffer losses when
prices continue to fall.

## 2. Long lookback requires years of data

The 756-day (3-year) lookback means the strategy produces no signals
for the first 3 years of data. Shorter histories or newly listed
stocks are excluded, introducing survivorship bias.

## 3. Proxy conflates with long-term reversal

The price-derived proxy (negative trailing 3-year return) captures
long-term reversal, which may not be identical to the book-to-market
effect. The strategy conflates value with contrarian reversal.

## 4. Value premium may have weakened post-2006

Multiple studies document a weakening of the HML value premium since
2006, particularly in US large-cap equities. The strategy may
underperform in recent out-of-sample periods.

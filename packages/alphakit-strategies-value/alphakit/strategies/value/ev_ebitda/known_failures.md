# Known failure modes — ev_ebitda

## 1. Capital structure dependency

EV/EBITDA is sensitive to capital structure — highly leveraged firms
have elevated enterprise values, distorting the multiple. The
price-only proxy does not capture debt-level differences across firms.

## 2. EBITDA ignores capex

EBITDA excludes capital expenditures, depreciation, and amortization.
Capital-intensive firms may appear cheap on EV/EBITDA but have low
free cash flow. The proxy inherits this blind spot.

## 3. Proxy limitation

The price-derived proxy (negative trailing 3-year return) is a noisy
stand-in for the actual enterprise multiple. It conflates value with
long-term reversal and cannot distinguish between operating-income
cheapness and price-based cheapness.

## 4. Financial sector distortion

EV/EBITDA is not meaningful for financial firms (banks, insurers)
whose capital structure and earnings are fundamentally different.
The original paper excludes financials; the price proxy does not.
